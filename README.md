# 💼 Income Prediction from Online Dating Profiles

---

## 📌 Overview
This project explores whether an individual’s **income** can be predicted using demographic and lifestyle variables from an online dating dataset. The analysis is performed in R and follows a complete data science workflow including exploration, modeling, and evaluation.

---

## 🎯 Objective
To investigate the relationship between income and variables such as age, sex, body type, diet, smoking status, relationship status, and height, and determine whether these features have predictive power.

---

## 🔍 Methodology
The project follows a structured data science pipeline:

- Data cleaning and preprocessing  
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
- Data visualization (histograms, boxplots, scatterplots)  
- Multiple linear regression modeling  
- Statistical significance testing  
- Diagnostic checks (linearity, normality, homoscedasticity)  
- Outlier and influence analysis (Cook’s distance, leverage)  
- Model selection (AIC, BIC, stepwise regression)  
- Log transformation of income for improvement  

---

## 📊 Key Findings
- Income distribution is strongly right-skewed  
- Most individuals earn relatively low income  
- Weak or no relationship between income and most predictors  
- Several variables are not statistically significant  
- The regression model has low explanatory power  
- Stepwise selection reduces the model to an intercept-only model  

---

## 🚀 Conclusion
The analysis shows that demographic and lifestyle variables alone are insufficient to predict income effectively. This suggests that income is influenced by more complex factors such as education level, occupation, and socioeconomic background that are not included in the dataset.
